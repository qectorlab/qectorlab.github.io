Commercial Licensing

License QECTOR for Commercial Use

QECTOR Decoder is source-available and free for personal, academic, and non-commercial research. Any commercial use — company R&D, products, SaaS, consulting, government, or funded research — requires a paid commercial license.

Who Needs a License

If any of these apply, a commercial license is required

The threshold is simple: if your organization earns revenue, is funded, or uses QECTOR to support any commercial objective, you need a paid license. Uncertainty is not a green light — reach out if you're unsure.

Company & Startup

Company or startup QEC work

Any use of QECTOR inside a for-profit entity — internal R&D, product evaluation, benchmarking for investor decks, or integration into commercial software — requires a commercial license. This includes pre-revenue startups with outside funding.

Quantum Hardware

Quantum hardware evaluation and integration

Using QECTOR to evaluate error correction performance on a real or simulated quantum processor — whether IBM, IonQ, Quantinuum, Pasqal, or custom hardware — constitutes commercial R&D and requires a license.

SaaS / Cloud

SaaS platform or hosted API

Offering a cloud service, hosted API, or software-as-a-service product that calls QECTOR decoding routines requires a SaaS commercial license, regardless of whether end users pay directly or indirectly.

OEM / Embedded

Hardware or firmware embedding

Embedding QECTOR Decoder in a quantum control system, FPGA-adjacent stack, or any hardware product for redistribution requires an OEM license with explicit redistribution rights.

Government / National Lab

Government and institutional deployment

Government agencies, national laboratories, and defence research organizations using QECTOR for any funded or operational purpose require a commercial license — academic exemptions do not apply to institutional deployment.

Consulting

Paid consulting or professional services

If you are being paid to provide technical services — benchmarking, integration advice, validation, or any professional deliverable — and QECTOR is part of that work, a commercial license is required for the duration of that engagement.

Funded Research

Grant-funded or institutionally funded research

Research funded by NSERC, NSF, DARPA, EU Horizon, or any public or private grant body that would benefit commercially from the results requires a commercial license. Pure academic publication without institutional commercialization interest may qualify for the free tier — contact to confirm.

Investor Validation

Fundraising and due-diligence use

Using QECTOR benchmark artifacts in investor materials, pitch decks, or technical due-diligence packages produced on behalf of a commercial entity constitutes commercial use and requires a license.

Not sure? The safe answer is to reach out before using QECTOR in a commercial context. Email admin@qector.store with a brief description of your use case. We respond within 2 business days.

What You License

Exactly what QECTOR Decoder includes

A commercial license grants you written authorization to use QECTOR Decoder for the defined commercial purposes. Here is what the decoder package includes that you're licensing.

Decoder backends

Exact MWPM (BlossomDecoder), belief-matching accuracy mode (BeliefMatching), Union-Find real-time path (UnionFindDecoder), SparseBlossomDecoder, BP-OSD for qLDPC codes, and CPU/GPU batch decoders — all in a single pip install.

Python ecosystem layer

Stim DEM loader, PyMatching-compatible drop-in API, Sinter plug-in (qector_blossom, qector_belief, qector_unionfind), AutoDecoder, GPU resilience and fallback, streaming decoder, and reproducible benchmark scaffolding.

Rust core

GIL-releasing, zero-copy NumPy interop. Union-Find with path compression, Blossom primal-dual, Rayon parallelism, NVRTC CUDA kernel compilation, OpenCL dual-kernel cross-vendor backend.

Validated test suite

414 tests — brute-force optimality checks, PyMatching parity regression through d=15, belief-matching cross-validation, BP-OSD syndrome faithfulness, GPU bit-identity, and Sinter harness integration.

Reproducible benchmark artifacts

SHA-256-hashed JSON/CSV artifacts, committed benchmark scripts, environment snapshot stamping, due-diligence bundle generator. Figures in the benchmark documentation trace to a specific build commit.

qLDPC support

BP-OSD decoder for bivariate-bicycle (BB), hypergraph-product, and bicycle CSS codes — code families that standard matching decoders cannot handle. Within ~10% LER of the reference ldpc package on [[72,12]] BB.

Licensing Process

How to get a commercial license

The process is direct — no sales funnel, no account managers. You describe your use case, we propose terms, you sign and pay, you're licensed. Typical turnaround from first email to signed license is 5–7 business days.

01

Submit a license inquiry

Use the contact form or email admin@qector.store with your organization name, intended use case, deployment scope, and tier you're targeting. The more specific, the faster the response.

02

Receive a license proposal

We respond within 2 business days with a license proposal covering permitted use, term, pricing, and any use-case-specific provisions. For Enterprise, SaaS, OEM, or Strategic discussions, an NDA can be signed first on request.

03

Review and negotiate terms

Standard license terms are non-negotiable at Startup tier. Professional and above allow limited customization of scope, multi-project provisions, and deployment clauses. Enterprise, SaaS, and OEM terms are fully negotiated.

04

Sign and pay

License agreement is signed digitally. Payment is by wire transfer, bank transfer, or invoice for qualified organizations. Annual licenses are invoiced at the start of each term. No auto-renewal without explicit written confirmation.

05

Receive your license document and begin use

A formal signed license document identifying your organization, permitted use, effective date, and term is delivered. Commercial use may begin as of the effective date stated in the license. Support access is activated immediately.

What to include in your inquiry

  • Organization name and type (startup, lab, enterprise, government)
  • Primary use case in one or two sentences
  • Deployment context (internal R&D, product, SaaS, OEM, or other)
  • Number of internal users who will interact with QECTOR directly
  • Whether GPU batch decoding is part of your requirements
  • Target license tier from the pricing page, or "not sure yet"
  • Preferred timeline to license start
Response time: All license inquiries receive an initial response within 2 business days. Québec statutory holidays may extend this by one day.
Do not begin commercial use before signing. Using QECTOR commercially without a signed license agreement constitutes a violation of the license terms and may result in termination of all license rights.
FAQ

Frequently asked licensing questions

Is QECTOR open-source?
No. QECTOR is source-available, not open-source. Source code is readable for review, benchmarking, and non-commercial use, but it is not licensed under any OSI-approved open-source license. Commercial use, redistribution, and embedding all require a paid commercial license.
Can I use QECTOR for academic research for free?
Pure academic research for personal study, non-commercial evaluation, and publishing results in peer-reviewed papers is permitted under the free tier. Research funded by a grant or institutional program that benefits the organization commercially requires a commercial license. When in doubt, reach out to confirm before beginning.
Do I need a license to benchmark QECTOR and publish results?
Non-commercial benchmarking and academic publishing is free. If benchmark results are used in investor materials, product marketing, or due-diligence documents for a commercial organization, a commercial license is required. Published results must preserve stated limitations, hardware details, and confidence intervals.
We're a VC-funded pre-revenue startup. Do we need a commercial license?
Yes. Outside-funded startups — regardless of revenue status — are commercial entities. The test is not revenue; it is commercial purpose. A seed-stage startup using QECTOR for product benchmarking or technical due-diligence needs the Startup tier.
Can we trial QECTOR commercially before purchasing?
A limited evaluation period can be included in a license proposal for Enterprise and above tiers. Startup and Professional tiers do not include an unpaid trial period — both tiers are priced for organizations that have already validated the technical fit using the free non-commercial tier.
What law governs the license agreement?
Unless otherwise specified in a custom commercial agreement, QECTOR licenses are governed by the laws of the Province of Québec and the applicable laws of Canada. Disputes are subject to the jurisdiction of the courts of Québec.
Can we negotiate custom license terms?
Startup tier uses standard non-negotiable terms. Professional tier allows limited customization of scope and deployment clauses. Enterprise, SaaS, OEM, and Strategic tiers are fully negotiated. All custom agreements require written sign-off from Guillaume Lessard / iD01t Productions.
Does a commercial license include QECTOR Workbench?
QECTOR Workbench is currently in development. When it launches, it will be included in Professional, Enterprise, SaaS, and OEM commercial licenses. Startup-tier licensees will have a separate upgrade path. Commercial licensees will receive early access ahead of the public release.
Can we embed QECTOR in a hardware product?
Embedding QECTOR in hardware, firmware, or quantum control systems for redistribution requires the OEM tier license ($250,000+/year), which includes explicit redistribution rights and IP provisions. Standard commercial tiers do not grant redistribution rights.
Is there a multi-year discount?
Multi-year commitments are available for Enterprise, SaaS, OEM, and Strategic tiers and are negotiated individually. Startup and Professional tiers are billed annually without a multi-year discount. Renewal pricing is locked at the rate in the original agreement for the first renewal term.
Strategic Discussions

Exclusive licensing, acquisition, and strategic partnerships

If you are interested in exclusive or semi-exclusive licensing, a joint development agreement, technology acquisition, or a strategic partnership — including potential IP transfer — these are handled separately from the standard commercial tiers. Discussions begin at $2M USD and are structured individually based on scope and strategic fit.

Exclusive licensing

QECTOR's exact MWPM and belief-matching decoder stack can be licensed exclusively for a specific market vertical, hardware platform, or geographic territory. Exclusivity terms are fully negotiated and time-bounded. Requires NDA prior to discussion.

Technology acquisition

Full or partial IP acquisition of the QECTOR decoder platform, including source code, benchmark methodology, test suite, and ongoing development commitments, is available for serious acquirers. All acquisition discussions begin under NDA.

Joint development

Co-development agreements for extending QECTOR to new code families, hardware-specific backends, or novel decoder architectures are available. These are structured as joint IP agreements with negotiated contribution and commercialization rights.

Investor-led strategic agreements

If your organization is leading or participating in a funding round that includes QECTOR as a strategic asset, contact directly. Investor-level discussions are handled under NDA and subject to valuation conversations.

All strategic and acquisition discussions are conducted under NDA. To initiate, email admin@qector.store with a one-paragraph description of the opportunity and your organization. NDA documents will be exchanged within 48 hours.
