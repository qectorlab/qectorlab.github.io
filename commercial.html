Commercial Licensing

When a Paid QECTOR License Makes Sense

QECTOR is free for personal, academic, and non-commercial research. Commercial use requires a paid license. Here’s when and why teams choose to pay.

Why Pay When PyMatching and Stim Are Free?

QECTOR is not positioned as the fastest single-shot MWPM decoder.

PyMatching remains the latency leader for exact MWPM on surface codes. Teams license QECTOR when they need broader capabilities, reproducibility, legal clarity, and workflow efficiency that free tools do not provide out of the box.

When teams choose QECTOR

  • Multi-decoder experiments (Union-Find, Blossom, Sparse Blossom, BP-OSD, streaming, batch, hybrid)
  • LDPC / qLDPC coverage with BP-OSD workflows beyond plain graph matching
  • Reproducible benchmark artifacts with SHA-256 hashes and environment snapshots
  • Written commercial license terms for company, lab, funded, product, hosted, or revenue-linked use
  • Paid support, validation packages, and technical advisory options

What commercial users actually get

  • Decoder breadth in one source-available platform
  • Reproducible evidence packaging (artifacts, scripts, manifests)
  • Commercial legal clarity with written license terms
  • Significant workflow time savings vs maintaining custom decoder stacks
  • Support path and validation options for commercial R&D
Who Needs a Commercial License

Clear rules for commercial use

Requires Paid License

  • Company or startup use (including pre-revenue funded startups)
  • Commercial R&D or product development
  • SaaS, hosted API, or cloud services
  • OEM embedding and redistribution
  • Government, national lab, or institutional deployment
  • Paid consulting or professional services
  • Grant-funded research with commercial benefit
  • Investor materials or due-diligence use

Free (Non-Commercial)

  • Personal learning and private experimentation
  • Academic research and evaluation
  • Educational demonstrations
  • Non-commercial benchmarking
  • Source code review
  • Publishing in peer-reviewed papers
