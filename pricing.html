Why pay when PyMatching and Stim are free?

QECTOR is not sold as the universal fastest MWPM decoder.

PyMatching remains the latency leader for exact MWPM on surface codes. QECTOR's commercial value comes from decoder breadth, reproducible evidence, legal clarity, and workflow time-saving — not from claiming to replace PyMatching in every scenario.

When teams choose QECTOR

  • Multi-decoder experiments (Union-Find, Blossom, Sparse Blossom, BP-OSD, streaming, batch, hybrid)
  • LDPC / qLDPC coverage with BP-OSD workflows
  • Reproducible benchmark artifacts with hashes and environment snapshots
  • Written commercial license for company, lab, funded, product, hosted, or revenue-linked use
  • Paid support, validation packages, and advisory options

What you actually get

  • Decoder diversity in one source-available platform
  • Reproducible evidence packaging (artifacts, scripts, manifests)
  • Commercial legal clarity and written license terms
  • Workflow time-saving vs maintaining custom decoder stacks
  • Support path for commercial R&D users

Pilot Success Criteria

A commercial pilot should be judged on measurable workflow value.

Recommended proof points for a successful evaluation:

Install success

Successful installation on the buyer's target machines with documented environment.

Reproduced benchmark

One reproduced benchmark artifact bundle with SHA-256 hashes.

QECTOR vs PyMatching

One head-to-head comparison run with documented results.

BP-OSD / LDPC workflow

One BP-OSD or LDPC workflow executed if relevant to the use case.

Artifact manifest

Documented artifact manifest with hashes and environment snapshot.

Time saved

Documented time saved versus existing PyMatching / Stim / custom workflow.
