Pricing that matches the product stage.

QECTOR is priced as a focused Rust/Python QEC R&D platform for multi-decoder experiments, reproducible benchmark workflows, BP-OSD/LDPC work, source access, and commercial legal permission. It is not priced as a mature real-time hardware QEC control stack.

Positioning

What buyers are paying for

Good fit

Teams that need a commercial R&D package: multiple decoder families, reproducible artifacts, source access, BP-OSD/LDPC paths, CPU/GPU batch workflows, and legal permission for internal commercial evaluation.

Weak fit

Teams that only need the fastest exact MWPM for standard surface-code workloads. PyMatching/Stim remain strong free alternatives and should be treated as the baseline comparator.

Notes

Claim and pricing discipline

Written permission required

A GitHub clone does not grant commercial rights. QECTOR is source-available, not open-source.

Conservative public pricing

The old $2M+ strategic floor was removed. Higher value terms need evidence and partner scope.

Final terms vary

User count, deployment model, redistribution rights, and support obligations can change pricing.

Upgrade path

Pricing can increase after throughput wins, hardware partnerships, enterprise features, or defensible IP.
